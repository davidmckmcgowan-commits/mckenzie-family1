The journey

Your family crossed the world over 75 years. Here is how they got here.

~1767
Invergordon, Scottish Highlands
Hugh McKenzie Sr is born in the rugged Highlands of northern Scotland. The McKenzie clan has deep roots in this landscape. Hugh grows up here before joining the army.
1794 – 1802
Ireland — fighting in a rebellion
Hugh joins the Caithness Legion and is sent to Ireland where a violent uprising against British rule is taking place. He serves over 7 years reaching the rank of Sergeant. Near Drogheda he meets Bridget Erskine and they marry around 1801.
"He served 7 years and 7 months, finishing as a sergeant." — family records
1803 – 1825
Kirkcudbright, Scotland
The family returns to Scotland. Children are born: John (1803), Hugh Jr (1804), Kenneth (1806) and others. Hugh works in coastal trading and possibly whaling.
April 1826
Saint John, New Brunswick, Canada
The McKenzies are victims of the Highland Clearances — rich landlords evicted thousands of Scottish families to make room for sheep. One source calls Hugh "an exile from Scotland." The family crosses the Atlantic and arrives in Canada. Within 10 days Hugh applies for 200 acres of land.
The Highland Clearances were one of the most painful events in Scottish history — whole communities were destroyed overnight.
1826 – 1840
Nova Scotia, Canada
The family builds a life in Canada. Hugh Jr becomes a ship captain sailing across the Atlantic. Baby John is born in April 1840 — just months before the next great voyage to Australia.
October 1840
Setting sail for Australia
The extended McKenzie family boards a ship called The Brothers and sets sail for the other side of the world. On board is 6-month-old baby John. The voyage takes about 2 months.
December 1840
Albany, Western Australia
The family arrives in Albany — a tiny settlement of around 200 people on the south coast of Western Australia. Tragically Hugh Sr dies just weeks later in January 1841 aged about 74. But the family stays and Albany becomes the McKenzie home for generations.
"When The Brothers arrived in Albany in December 1840, on board was eight-month old Canadian born John." — family history
1840 onwards
Building Albany
The McKenzies do not just live in Albany — they help build it. Hugh Jr runs the Ship Inn. John becomes a whaler at 14, then buys the Freemasons Hotel, becomes Mayor three times, fights for public rights to the foreshore and is remembered as one of the town's founding fathers.

Cool stories

Your ancestors had remarkable lives. Here are the most extraordinary moments.

⚔
The soldier who crossed the world
Hugh Sr fought in a real rebellion then somehow ended up in Western Australia.

Hugh McKenzie Sr joined the Caithness Legion and was sent to Ireland during the Irish Rebellion of 1798 — a violent uprising against British rule. He fought, survived and served for over 7 years reaching the rank of Sergeant.

After leaving the army he returned to Scotland, had a family, got thrown off his land in the Highland Clearances and moved to Canada. Then in his 70s he got on yet another ship to Australia.

He arrived in Albany in December 1840 and died just weeks later in January 1841 aged about 74. He had lived on three continents and crossed the Atlantic twice.

"An exile from Scotland" — how one source described Hugh Sr after the Highland Clearances
🚢
Ship captain Hugh Jr
Your ancestor commanded real ships across oceans and got shot at in his own hotel.

Hugh McKenzie Jr became a professional ship captain. He commanded the Rob Roy in 1833 sailing from Ireland to Liverpool with coal and salt. In 1834 he captained the Trial at Kingston Jamaica. In 1836 he mastered the Fenwick Keating.

After arriving in Albany he leased the Ship Inn — a pub right on the waterfront. He had a white cockatoo above the bar that had been taught to say rude things to customers.

"Hello! Drunk again? Get out, you black cow!" — the cockatoo's favourite greeting

One day a French whaling captain was not impressed and took a shot at Hugh in his own hotel. Hugh survived.

🐋
John the teenage whaler
At just 14 John went out whale hunting to support his widowed mother.

When John's father died in 1854 his mother was widowed with young children at home. John was just 14. He left school and went to work on whaling ships at Cheyne Beach Station 40 miles east of Albany.

Whaling was incredibly dangerous. Once a whale was spotted the crew rowed out in small boats to harpoon it. A wounded whale could drag the boat at speed for hours or turn and attack.

John worked in whaling for 22 years. In 1876 a whale attacked his boat killing two of his crew and fracturing several of John's ribs. He had to stop. He was 36 years old.

"He had a bad mishap while whaling at Doubtful Island Bay. Two men in his boat were killed by a small whale which attacked the boat. John was knocked overboard but scrambled back on." — Captain Sale's memoirs
🏪
The hotel dynasty
Three generations of McKenzies ran pubs in early Albany.

The McKenzies were central to Albany's social life for decades through their hotels. Hugh Jr leased the Ship Inn in the 1840s right on the waterfront. His son John bought the Freemasons Hotel in 1870 for 1000 pounds.

In 1904 John spent nearly 3000 pounds on a massive renovation turning it into a three storey hotel described as being in the Italian Renaissance style. It had 56 to 60 rooms.

"In raising this fine building he has manifested a public spirit and a belief in the future of Albany." — Albany newspaper 1905
🪓
John chops down the fence
When a company fenced off Albany's foreshore John grabbed an axe.

In the late 1880s a company built a railway line to Albany along the waterfront blocking the townspeople's access to their own harbour. The company started erecting fences on public streets.

John McKenzie decided to take direct action.

"John McKenzie personally went to the foot of Bridges Street with an axe and chopped down a fence which had been illegally erected by the W.A. Land Company, thus starting a row which terminated with the people of Albany asserting their right to be masters in their own town." — Albany memoir

The town council took the company to the Supreme Court and won. John was a key witness in the case.

⚖
Ernest the goldfields engineer
From railway workshops to the gold rush — Ernest had the most varied career.

Ernest George McKenzie born in 1877 started working at the Albany Railway workshops aged 18. In 1897 he headed to the goldfields at Norseman. He then worked on ships for the Adelaide Steamship Company.

In 1901 he graduated from the School of Mines in Adelaide passing exams in assaying chemistry machine design and metalwork. He became a qualified mining engineer.

While studying in Adelaide he met Isabelle Niven Pearson who was an accomplished musician and artist. They married in Albany in 1904.

🌋
Johnny sails past Krakatoa
John's son was at sea when the most destructive volcanic eruption in history happened.

John Edward McKenzie Jr left Albany in 1883 aged 17 with his brother-in-law James Clark. They sailed north to establish a trading business at the mouth of the Ashburton River.

As they arrived at the Ashburton River...

"The great Java Straits earthquake took place causing a tidal wave to rush up the river and resulting in a great commotion." — family history

This was the eruption of Krakatoa — one of the most violent volcanic events ever recorded. The explosion was heard 5000 kilometres away and killed over 36000 people. Johnny and James would have felt the tidal wave surge up the river around their boat.

Despite this dramatic beginning their business Clarke and McKenzie became very successful. Sadly Johnny died in Onslow in 1897 aged just 31.

Family mysteries

Every family has unanswered questions. Here are the things your family is still trying to figure out. Maybe you can help solve them one day!

When was Hugh Sr actually born?
His tombstone suggests 1766 but land grant documents say 1769. Even the family historians are not sure.
Who did Hugh Laurie marry?
John's letter from 1882 says "Hugh is married" but no marriage record has ever been found. His wife may have died before him.
What happened to Emily?
Emily was Hugh Jr's daughter born around 1832. She simply disappears from records completely. Did she move interstate? Nobody knows.
Was Isabelle Hind from Scotland or England?
Hugh Jr's wife is described in one source as being born in Preston Lancashire in England. But the family came from Scotland. We do not yet know where she was really from.
Was Leslie actually Sabina's child?
Albert's daughter Leslie Evelyn has duplicate birth certificates — one naming Albert's wife Catherine as the mother and one naming Albert's sister Sabina. Nobody knows for sure.

If you know anything that could solve these mysteries please share it with the family. Every small detail can unlock a big discovery.
